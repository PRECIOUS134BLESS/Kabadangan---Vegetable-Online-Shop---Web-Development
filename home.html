In our region, the agriculture sector faces a pressing issue of oversupply, particularly with vegetables. Favorable growing conditions lead to high yields, and with many farmers growing similar crops, we end up with more produce than can be consumed or sold locally. This surplus drives prices down significantly, forcing farmers to sell their produce at lower prices to avoid spoilage, which severely impacts their income. The situation is further complicated by the involvement of middlemen—wholesalers and retailers—who buy vegetables from farmers at low prices and sell them at a higher cost, taking a significant share of the profits. This system leaves farmers struggling to earn a fair income despite their abundant production.

Mission

KABADANGAN helps build a better way to share food. It connects farmers with extra crops to people who need them, reducing waste and making sure farmers earn fair prices.

The platform allows farmers to sell fresh, healthy food directly to customers at reasonable prices. At the same time, it gives families access to affordable, locally grown vegetables.

KABADANGAN’s goal is to support farmers, provide good food for communities, and create a stronger, fairer food system for everyone.
